Belarus

The Land of Lakes, Castles and Welcoming People

About Belarus

Belarus is a country in Eastern Europe with a rich history, unique culture, and beautiful nature. It borders Russia, Ukraine, Poland, Lithuania, and Latvia. The capital of Belarus is Minsk, one of the most beautiful cities in the region known for its clean streets and Soviet-era architecture.

Flag of Belarus

The current flag of Belarus is a red-and-green flag with a white-and-red ornament pattern placed at the hoist (staff) end. The red color symbolizes the past struggles of the Belarusian people, the green represents life and the forests of Belarus, and the ornament represents the cultural heritage and spiritual continuity of the nation.

Main Attractions

Belarus boasts numerous historical and natural attractions that attract tourists from all over the world. From ancient castles to pristine forests, there is something for every traveler.

Mir Castle

A 16th-century architectural monument included in the UNESCO World Heritage List. Located in the town of Mir in the Grodno Region, this Gothic-style castle is one of Belarus's most iconic landmarks.

Bialowieża Forest

An ancient forest, one of the largest and last remaining parts of the immense primeval forest that once stretched across the European Plain. It is home to European bison, and the reserve is a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

Nesvizh Castle

A palace-castle complex from the 16th-17th centuries, the residence of the Radziwill princes. This magnificent Renaissance-Baroque castle is surrounded by beautiful gardens and is a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

Culture and Traditions

Belarusian culture is a unique blend of Slavic traditions with influences from neighboring countries. The country has a rich heritage of folk art, music, literature, and cuisine.

Cuisine

Belarusian cuisine is famous for draniki (potato pancakes), kolduny (dumplings), machanka (pork stew) and other dishes made from potatoes, meat and mushrooms. Traditional beverages include kvas and berezavik (birch sap).

Holidays

Important holidays include Victory Day, Independence Day, Kupalle (Ivan Kupala Day) and other folk and state holidays. Traditional celebrations often involve folk music, dancing, and elaborate rituals.

Language and Literature

Belarusian literature has rich traditions, from Francysk Skaryna to contemporary writers. The language belongs to the East Slavic group. Notable authors include Vasil Bykau, Svetlana Alexievich (Nobel Prize winner), and Yanka Kupala.

Folk Art

Rich tradition of weaving, pottery, and wood carving

Geography of Belarus

Topography

Belarus is predominantly flat, with the highest point being Dzyarzhynskaya Hara at 345 meters (1,132 ft). The country features extensive marshlands, particularly in the southern regions. About 40% of Belarus is covered by forests, earning it the nickname "the lungs of Europe".

Water Resources

Belarus has over 20,000 rivers and 11,000 lakes. Major rivers include the Dnieper (which flows to the Black Sea), Neman (to the Baltic Sea), Western Dvina (to the Baltic Sea), and Pripyat (a tributary of the Dnieper). The largest lake is Narach, covering 79.6 square kilometers.

Natural Regions

The country can be divided into three main geographical regions: the northern lakelands, the central plains, and the southern marshlands (Polesye). The Belavezhskaya Pushcha National Park, a UNESCO World Heritage site, contains Europe's last remaining primeval forest.

Climate

Belarus has a moderate continental climate with cold winters (average January temperature: -4°C to -8°C) and cool, moist summers (average July temperature: 17°C to 19°C). The country receives moderate precipitation throughout the year, with the highest amounts occurring during summer.

Biodiversity

Belarus is home to diverse flora and fauna, including European bison (the national animal), elk, deer, wild boar, wolves, lynx, and beavers. The country has several national parks and nature reserves protecting its unique ecosystems.

Location & Borders

Belarus is a landlocked country in Eastern Europe, bordered by Russia to the northeast, Ukraine to the south, Poland to the west, and Lithuania and Latvia to the northwest. It lies at the crossroads of major transportation routes between Western Europe and Russia.
